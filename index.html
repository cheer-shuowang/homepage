About

Hi! I'm Wang Shuo (王硕), you can also call me Allen. I'm an interdisciplinary researcher working at the intersection of Game Studies, Computational Social Science, and AI & Society. I use data-driven methods — NLP, machine learning, and agent-based modeling — to understand how digital communities shape identity, discourse, and public opinion.

Currently, I'm a Master's student at the National University of Singapore (NUS), while concurrently pursuing an M.S. in Artificial Intelligence (Online) at the University of Colorado Boulder.

Before academia, I worked as a product and strategy intern at Bilibili, Tencent, and Weibo, where I witnessed firsthand how algorithms and platform design shape user behavior — experiences that now fuel my research questions.

My research focuses on:

  • Game Studies — Player communities, identity politics, sentiment dynamics in live service games
  • AI & Society — Toxicity perception, content moderation, algorithmic influence on public discourse
  • Data Science — NLP pipelines, ML-based prediction, spatial-temporal modeling for urban analytics
Technical: Python (NLP, ML, Data Analysis) · SQL · Stata · SPSS · GIS & Spatial Analysis · Git
Languages: Mandarin (native) · English (advanced) · Spanish (intermediate)

Outside of research, I enjoy traveling, playing the violin, and immersing myself in the virtual worlds of video games.
